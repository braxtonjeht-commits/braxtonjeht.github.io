Operations

Screenshot in. Route intel out.

No spreadsheets, no manual entry, no dropdowns. The bot reads the terminal screen for you — station, prices, stock, all of it.

STEP 01 · IN-GAME

Screenshot the terminal

At any trade terminal, capture the BUY tab and the LOCAL MARKET VALUE tab. Order doesn't matter — the bot auto-detects which is which.

STEP 02 · DISCORD

Run /scan

Attach both screenshots. OCR pulls the station name, every commodity, every price, and every stock level — then merges it into the network in seconds.

STEP 03 · PAYOUT

Get personalized routes

You're DM'd fresh routes sized to your registered ship, and your server's pinned route board updates for everyone. Use /haul and /sell any time.

The unlock economy

Intel is earned, not given.

Every server unlocks its route board by contributing scans. That's not a paywall — it's what keeps the data fresh for everyone on the network.

  • 1 unique station scanned unlocks your first live route. Three stations unlocks the full board.
  • Daily quests assign each scanner the stalest terminal on the network — coverage goes where it's needed.
  • Banked days reward overachievers: extra scans bank up to 3 days of access for your slow weeks.
  • 48-hour expiry means a dead server gets stale routes, and an active one never does.

Questions from the hangar

Is it free?

Yes. Add it to your server, scan a terminal, get routes. The network runs on contributed scans, not subscriptions.

What stops people from submitting fake prices?

Several layers: duplicate and near-duplicate screenshot detection, per-terminal cooldowns, rate limiting, and price sanity checks against UEX — a scan claiming a price wildly out of line with known data is rejected and flagged. Bad actors get noticed fast.

Does it work in Pyro and Nyx?

Yes — all three systems. Gateway stations even get a quick "which side are you on?" prompt so jump-point scans land in the right system.

What permissions does the bot need?

Just enough to create one #sc-intel channel, post and pin its route board, and read slash commands. It never reads your server's messages — it doesn't even request that permission.

What does my org actually see?

A pinned, auto-updating route board, a contributor leaderboard, and a scan feed. Each scanner also gets private DM results with routes sized to their registered ship via /setship.

Who builds this?
